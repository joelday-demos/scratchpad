Gifts, Hospitality, and Procurement Integrity Policy

Government official interactions
Employees must avoid offering, giving, soliciting, or accepting anything of value that could appear to influence a government official or contractor representative. This includes meals, entertainment, travel, gifts, discounts, favors, and personal services.

Active procurements and source selections
During an active procurement, bid evaluation, source selection, protest period, or negotiation, hospitality involving government acquisition personnel is high risk. Employees must not provide meals, entertainment, gifts, transportation, or favors to contracting officers, contracting officer representatives, source selection officials, evaluators, or program officials connected to the procurement unless Compliance and Legal have approved in advance.

Nominal items
Company-branded items of nominal value may be allowed only when permitted by law, customer rules, and internal policy. Even nominal items are not allowed if they could appear to influence an active procurement or award decision.

Procurement-sensitive and competitor information
Employees must not request, receive, use, or distribute source selection information, contractor bid or proposal information, non-public competitor pricing, non-public technical evaluations, or information that appears to have been obtained through a government role or another contractor's confidential relationship.

Former government employees and consultants
If a former government employee, consultant, advisor, teammate, subcontractor, or customer contact offers non-public procurement or competitor information, employees must stop the conversation, preserve the facts, and contact Legal or Compliance. Do not open attachments, forward the information, or use it for capture, pricing, solution design, or proposal strategy until cleared.

Examples
High risk: Buying lunch for a contracting officer while the company's proposal is under evaluation.
High risk: Accepting competitor labor rates from a consultant who obtained them in a prior government role.
Medium risk: Providing a nominal branded pen at an industry day when no active procurement decision is pending and customer rules allow it.
Low risk: Attending a customer-hosted public industry event where all offerors have equal access to the same information.
